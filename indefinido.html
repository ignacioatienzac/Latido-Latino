The Preterite Tense (Pretérito Indefinido)

Regular Verbs

The Preterite Tense is formed by adding specific endings to the verb's stem. The endings vary depending on whether the verb ends in -ar, -er, or -ir.

Endings for -AR verbs (Example: hablar)

Regular Verbs with Spelling Changes

To preserve the original sound of the verb's infinitive, some regular -AR verbs require a spelling change. This change only happens in the first-person singular (Yo) form. The other forms are regular.

Verbs ending in -CAR (c → qu)

To maintain the hard /k/ sound before an 'e', the 'c' changes to 'qu'.

Common verbs:

  • buscar (to look for), sacar (to take out), tocar (to touch/play), practicar (to practice), explicar (to explain)
Conjugation of buscar
Yobusqué
Túbuscaste
Él/Ella/Ud.buscó
Nosotros/asbuscamos
Vosotros/asbuscasteis
Ellos/Ellas/Uds.buscaron

Verbs ending in -GAR (g → gu)

To maintain the hard /g/ sound before an 'e', a 'u' is added.

Common verbs:

  • llegar (to arrive), jugar (to play), pagar (to pay), apagar (to turn off), colgar (to hang)
Conjugation of llegar

Verbs ending in -ZAR (z → c)

To maintain the soft 's' sound, the 'z' changes to 'c' before an 'e'.

Common verbs:

  • empezar (to start), almorzar (to have lunch), organizar (to organize), lanzar (to throw), cruzar (to cross)

Irregular Verbs: Vowel Changes

A specific group of -IR verbs are irregular because they have a vowel change in their stem. This change only happens in the third person (él/ella/usted and ellos/ellas/ustedes). The endings are regular.

Change e → i

Example: pedir (to ask for)

Yopedí
Túpediste
Él/Ella/Ud.pidió
Nosotros/aspedimos
Vosotros/aspedisteis
Ellos/Ellas/Uds.pidieron

Change o → u

Example: dormir (to sleep)

Yodormí
Túdormiste
Él/Ella/Ud.durmió
Nosotros/asdormimos
Vosotros/asdormisteis
Ellos/Ellas/Uds.durmieron

Irregular Verbs: Root and Ending

This group of verbs is completely irregular. They have an irregular stem and also use a unique set of endings, which are different from regular verbs.

Endings for "strong" irregulars:

-e, -iste, -o, -imos, -isteis, -ieron / -eron*

*"-eron" is used when the stem ends in 'j' (e.g., dijeron).
